WHAT IS ABA?

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) therapy is a scientifically validated approach that uses techniques rooted in behavioral psychology to help individuals learn new skills and improve their behavior. ABA therapy is designed to address a wide range of developmental and behavioral challenges by breaking down complex behaviors into smaller, more manageable parts and teaching these skills through positive reinforcement and repetition. The goal of ABA therapy is to help individuals develop practical skills and behaviors that can be applied in their daily lives, leading to more independence and better quality of life.

Frequently Asked Questions

ABA therapy can be appropriate for individuals of all ages, from young children to adults.

ABA therapy can be used to address a wide range of challenges, including communication difficulties, social skills deficits, problem behaviors, and academic challenges.

The length of ABA therapy varies depending on the individual's needs and goals. Some individuals may only need a few months of therapy, while others may require several years.

Many insurance plans cover ABA therapy, but it's important to check with your insurance provider to see what is covered under your specific plan.

ABA therapy is a behaviorally-based approach that focuses on the principles of learning and reinforcement. It is typically more structured and goal-oriented than other forms of therapy.

You can start by asking your doctor for a referral or searching online for ABA therapy providers in your area. It's important to choose a provider who is licensed and experienced in working with individuals with your specific needs.
